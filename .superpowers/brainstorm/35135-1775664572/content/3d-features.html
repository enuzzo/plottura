Which 3D features matter most?

This helps us evaluate which technology best fits your vision. Select all that apply.

1

3D Buildings

Extruded buildings with real heights from OpenStreetMap data. Skyscrapers are tall, houses are short. Flat colored faces, no textures — like a minimalist architectural model.

2

Terrain / Elevation

Real elevation data — mountains rise, valleys dip, coastlines gain depth. The map surface is no longer flat. Works great for locations with dramatic geography (Alps, fjords, canyons).

3

Lighting & Shadows

Directional light casting shadows from buildings/terrain. Adds depth and atmosphere. Think "golden hour" lighting on a city skyline. Makes the 3D feel real instead of flat-shaded.

4

Adjustable Tilt Angle

A slider or control to adjust how much the map tilts (0° = flat top-down, 60° = dramatic perspective). The user decides the viewing angle per poster.

Why this matters for tech choice

MapLibre Native

Buildings ✓, Terrain ✓, Lighting ✓ (v5), Pitch ✓. Already in your stack. Zero new deps. But: locked to its own rendering pipeline — less creative control.

Three.js

Full 3D scene control. Custom lighting, materials, camera. But: you'd need to extract map data and rebuild the scene yourself. ~150KB dep. Heavy integration work.

Deck.gl

Geospatial 3D layer on top of map engines. Great building extrusions, terrain. Integrates with MapLibre. ~200KB but purpose-built for this exact use case.
