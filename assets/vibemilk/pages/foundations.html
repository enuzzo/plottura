02b - Typography Specimen

Golden Ratio Hierarchy & Layout

All spacing, line-heights and proportions follow the golden ratio (φ = 1.618). Spacing between heading and body = heading-size × 0.618. Line-height = size × 1.618.

Golden Ratio Reference
A = 1
B = 0.618
A + B = φ (1.618) · A / B = φ · Applied to all spacing, sizing, and proportions below
Heading Hierarchy (real spacing)
Case Study

Design is how
it works

Every pixel, every proportion, every breath of white space is intentional. The golden ratio isn't a trend — it's mathematics made visible.

The Modular Scale

Typography is the voice of design. When type sizes relate to each other through the golden ratio, hierarchy becomes effortless. The eye moves naturally from display to heading to body, each step a φ-derived proportion smaller than the last.

A well-set page doesn't need decoration. The mathematics of proportion — the same mathematics that govern spiral galaxies and nautilus shells — create beauty through structure alone.

Vertical Rhythm

The space after a heading equals the heading's font-size multiplied by 0.618 — the inverse golden ratio. This creates an intimate coupling between heading and content while maintaining enough air to breathe.

Paragraph Spacing

Paragraphs are separated by the body line-height × 1.0, creating a consistent vertical grid. Every spacing value in the system derives from a φ-based calculation.

Spacing annotations: heading → body = fs × 0.618 · paragraph gap = line-height × 1.0 · section gap = display × φ
